How it works
Signal, not speculation
Every prediction is derived from the public regulatory record. When CBIC clarifies the same topic twice, the third clarification is already in the pipeline. When a GST Council item gets deferred, it returns. When an AAR ruling creates a judicial split, CBIC intervenes. These patterns are consistent and measurable.
Signal types
Five evidence layers
Circular frequency — repeated CBIC clarifications on the same topic signal ongoing ambiguity.

Council deferral — deferred agenda items resurface with near certainty.

AAR density — 3+ rulings on one topic in 12 months precede CBIC intervention.

Budget language — specific phrases reliably precede council action.

Industry demand — repeated asks across memoranda signal eventual response.
Calibration
Backtested on
2017–present
The model is validated against known historical changes. Predictions in the 50–70% range materialise ~65% of the time. Predictions above 70% materialise ~82% of the time. All predictions link to the raw signals. No black boxes.
